Intubox

A Device Built by Columbia Engineers and Doctors to Protect Healthcare Workers During Intubation of COVID-19 Patients

Download Contribute

Built with Bootstrap 4

The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the need to protect clinicians from exposure during aerosolizing procedures--especially given the scarcity of personal protective equipment (PPE). We propose a clear, reusable, protective case to be placed around the patientâ€™s head during such procedures to prevent splash. We have developed an a prototype and are making the design and specs available for anyone to use.

Download

Our design is publically available and free for download. The box is designed to be made using readily available materials and equipment available in "maker spaces". With appropriate materials, two people can make about ten boxes per day. In addition, a document providing detailed user testing is linked below. Our prototypes were constructed by asymptomatic individuals wearing masks, and each unit was thoroughly cleaned with 70% Ethanol which was allowed to dry.
